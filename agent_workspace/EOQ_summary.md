# Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) Model Summary

## Definition
The Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) model is a fundamental inventory management tool that determines the optimal order quantity a company should purchase to minimize total inventory costs, which include ordering and holding costs.

## Key Components
1. **Demand Rate**: The rate at which inventory is used or sold over a specific period.
2. **Ordering Cost**: The fixed cost incurred every time an order is placed, regardless of the order size.
3. **Holding Cost**: The cost to hold or store inventory over a period, typically expressed as a percentage of the inventory value.

## EOQ Formula
The EOQ can be calculated using the formula:

\[ EOQ = \sqrt{\frac{2DS}{H}} \]

Where:
- \(D\) = Demand rate (units per period)
- \(S\) = Ordering cost per order
- \(H\) = Holding cost per unit per period

## Benefits of EOQ
- **Cost Minimization**: Helps in reducing total inventory costs by balancing ordering and holding costs.
- **Improved Cash Flow**: By optimizing order quantities, businesses can improve their cash flow management.
- **Inventory Control**: Provides a systematic approach to inventory management, reducing the risk of stockouts or overstocking.

## Limitations
- **Constant Demand Assumption**: EOQ assumes that demand is constant, which may not be realistic in all scenarios.
- **Static Costs**: The model assumes that ordering and holding costs remain constant, which may not hold true in dynamic markets.

## Conclusion
The EOQ model is a valuable tool for businesses looking to optimize their inventory management practices. By understanding and applying the EOQ formula, companies can effectively manage their inventory levels, reduce costs, and improve overall operational efficiency.
